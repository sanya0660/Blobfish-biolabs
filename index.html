Our mission

Blobfish BioLabs is a youth-led Bay Area-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization. We host 4-week hands-on biology programs for children 3rd through 8th grade.

Questions

How are we special?
In each lesson, kids get to apply their knowledge through experiments and plan out in-class projects. By registering your kids, you help fund local hospitals: UCSF Benioff Children's Hospital, Lucile Packard Children's Hospital, and more.
How long are classes?
For elementary schoolers (3rd - 5th grade), classes run from 2:00 - 3:00pm. For middle schoolers (6th - 8th grade), classes run from 3:15 - 4:30pm.
When and where are the classes?
Classes are located at Independence High School, Room D202, every Saturday.

Volunteers

Why tutor?

As a tutor at Blobfish BioLabs, you'll help kids learn about biology in real time while earning community service hours. Your tasks as a member include:

  • Attending normal program or special events
  • Leading a group of 4-6 kids during slideshows and experiments
  • Setting up and cleaning up our event venue
  • Creating online project and experiment slideshows for our curriculum

Log your hours

Log your hours as soon as your task has ended to help confirmation go smoothly. Give us up to two days to email you a confirmation. If you haven't received the email, please contact blobfishbiolabs@gmail.com.

Blobfish BioLabs is a Bay Area-based outreach organization primarily led by high school students. It hosts 4-week biology programs for elementary school (3rd - 5th grade) and middle school students (6th - 8th grade), where they learn important medical and anatomical concepts through slideshows and experiments. Extra funds raised support local hospitals, including UCSF Benioff Children's Hospital and Lucile Packard Children's Hospital.

Blobfish BioLabs' main mission is to introduce science, medical, and biology concepts to the Bay Area community through inexpensive programs that feature interactive experiments. BioLabs targets primarily low-income and underserved areas, while welcoming people from all backgrounds to participate in our programs.
